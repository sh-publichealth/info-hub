The Recruitment Process

Part 1. First contact

NoteRecruitment Part 1 Notes

R1. Use the REDCap “Recruitment Report”. Choose and contact the next person on the list.

R2/R3. For potential participants responding to the project advert or recruited from clinic, search the REDCap database to see if they exist. If not on the database, create and complete a new Screening Record for this person.

R4. Search the REDCap database for the person in question. You will use the person’s REDCap Screening Form during the recruitment process.

Part 2. Participant Choices

NoteRecruitment Part 2 Notes

R5. You will only make contact if the person already meets the age, diabetes duration, and recent clinic visit criteria (see Eligibility for details). They will still need height and weight measurement (for the calculation of Body Mass Index) and an eating disorder check (see below for both) to confirm final eligibility.

R6. If interested, book a screening visit using the REDCap visit scheduling tool. Point the person to the online Information Sheet.

R7. If possibly interested, point the participant to the online Information Sheet. And schedule a follow-up call.

R8. If not interested, thank the person for their time.

R9. For each possible outcome, updates to the REDCap Screening Form will be required. Follow the REDCap guidance on this.

The Screening Visit

Part 1. Screening Visit Preparation

NoteScreening Part 1 Notes

S1. During this pre-visit work, you will need to update the screening form for each potential participant.

S2. The medical notes review is an important step to identify clinical factors that might exclude someone from taking part in this intervention. Potential exclusion factors are as follows:

  • Active mental illness
  • AMI or stroke in past 3 months
  • Severe / unstable heart failure
  • Porphyria
  • Pregnant until >4 months post-partum or breastfeeding
  • Substance abuse
  • Eating disorder accompanied by purging
  • eGFR <30mls/min/1.73 within previous 3 months

Part 2. At the Screening Visit (BMI check)

NoteScreening Part 2 Notes

The REDCap screening form should be completed during the screening visit. Follow the screening Visit SOP and also use the screening checklist to ensure all required steps are completed.

S5 / S6. For the identity check and the TMR overview, follow the screening visit notes.

S7. Height and Weight should be measured following the simple protocol. Results must be entered directly into the REDCap Screening form, which will provide you with the person’s Body Mass Index for you to assess eligibility.

S8. The REDCap Screening Form will also use the already-completed fields to assess and complete overall eligibility based on four criteria (Age, Diabetes Duration, Clinic Visit Recency, BMI). Use this REDCap eligibility classification to guide your next steps.

Part 3. At the Screening Visit (Eating Disorder check)

NoteScreening Part 3 Notes

S10/S11/S13. These steps collectively form the eating disorder screening process. You should follow this process carefully as set out in the eating disorder workflow, filling out the question responses in REDCap, and using the resulting REDCap scores to dictate the eventual participant pathway.

The Baseline Visit

Part 1. Before Data Collection Begins

NoteBaseline Part 1 Notes

B2. As part of this visit preamble, you should:

  • Confirm the identity of the participant
  • Confirm their eligibility status
  • Provide a brief review of the TMR intervention

B3. The participant must understand the following before being asked to sign the consent form:

  • Review how we plan to use the data
  • Review our data confidentiality guarantee
  • Review our plans for data use

B4. Obtain informed consent via the REDCap eConsent process. See the REDCap guidance for details.

Part 2. Data Collection

NoteBaseline Part 2 Notes

B5. The REDCap baseline form should be completed during the baseline visit. Follow the Baseline Visit SOP and also use the baseline checklist to ensure all required steps are completed.

B6. Anthropometry includes:

  • weight
  • waist circumference
  • hip circumference

B7. Clinical Measures includes:

  • blood pressure
  • glucose monitoring

B8. Blood sample will be used for:

  • HbA1C
  • liver panel

B11. Patient Reported Outcomes include:

  • Patient-Reported Outcomes
  • Eq5D - quality of life
  • PAID - diabetes distress
  • IPAQ - exercise
